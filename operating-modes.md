This document defines how the Inkfinity Platform is used at different stages
of the business lifecycle.

The platform is production-ready by design, but intentionally gated while
validation, refinement, and proof-of-use are gathered.

---

## Mode 1: Founder-Only (Current)

Status: ACTIVE  
Public access: Disabled  

Description:
The platform is operated exclusively by the founder to create hubs on behalf
of customers.

Use cases:
- Rapid hub creation for in-person sales
- Button and CTA suggestion testing
- Refining onboarding questions
- Identifying common customer gaps
- Building real-world proof and case studies

Characteristics:
- No self-serve onboarding
- No public user accounts
- Founder controls all hub creation
- Customers receive the finished hub only

Purpose:
To validate logic, reduce future support burden, and ensure high-quality hubs
before opening public access.

---

## Mode 2: Assisted Onboarding (Planned)

Status: PLANNED  
Public access: Limited (guided only)  

Description:
The platform is used live with customers, but still operated by the founder.

Use cases:
- In-person or video onboarding
- Guided data entry
- Real-time AI suggestions
- Faster hub setup with customer present

Characteristics:
- No autonomous self-serve flow
- Founder-led sessions
- Customers participate but do not operate system alone

Purpose:
To validate usability, refine AI suggestions, and gather conversion evidence
before full self-serve launch.

---

## Mode 3: Self-Serve (Future)

Status: FUTURE  
Public access: Enabled  

Description:
The platform is opened to customers for independent onboarding and hub creation.

Use cases:
- Online sign-up
- Automated onboarding
- Subscription-based access

Characteristics:
- Plan-based limits enforced automatically
- AI suggestions enabled by plan
- Reduced founder involvement

Purpose:
To scale the platform after proof, refinement, and market validation.

---

## Key Principle

The Inkfinity Platform is not released based on technical readiness alone,
but on confidence in customer outcomes.

Deliberate gating is a strategic decision, not a technical limitation.
